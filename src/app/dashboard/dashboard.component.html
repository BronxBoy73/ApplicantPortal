Before Interview Day
Directions to Einstein
See travel directions. You can get to the campus by driving, subway, or bus from Manhattan, or cab, or by Airtrain from Newark or JFK airports. (LaGuardia is the closest airport.) We suggest however, that if you take the Airtrain from JFK, that at Jamaica Station, you take the E subway to the Lexington Ave/53 Street stop. At Lexington Ave/53 Street, transfer to the “6” train (through the 51 Street Station) and egress at Westchester Square. From there, take the #31 or #21 bus to Eastchester Road and Morris Park Avenue. The #10 express bus (BXM10) from Manhattan stops in front of the Belfer Building. For travel by express bus, please be sure to get a Metrocard when you arrive in NY, or have $6.50 in quarters available; NYC buses do not accept paper money. Cabs to the College from the airport can be costly, so please be prepared. Also, please be sure that you take a yellow city cab, not a private cab, unless you’ve called for a private cab in advance or Uber. The day of your interview, you can ask Mary Pace if there are other applicants going back to the airport. If you decide that you would like to drive, there are two options. Einstein is a Zipcar site, and Enterprise offers a discounted Einstein rate for the day if you use the code F22547 when you book your reservation. You are liable for insurance. Einstein/Yeshiva University is not responsible for accidents or damage to vehicles; you would be completely responsible. You must show a photo ID, and inform Enterprise beforehand that it will not be a YU/Einstein ID. If spaces are available, you may park in the Quik Park Garage that is one block from the Belfer Building, telephone: 718-430-7021 for not more than $12 per day. Hours are 6:30 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Also, there is a 24-hour Einstein hospital parking lot on Sacket Avenue on Eastchester Road, opposite the Weiler Hospital, a short walk to the Belfer Building. For the location of both, see the campus map.
Hotel Accommodations
The Residence Inn Marriott at the Metro Center Atrium right across the street from Einstein is new and beautiful, and each suite features a separate living and work area and a full kitchen. The corporate booking code to access discounted rates is YUJ. This code can be entered on the hotel website ( www.marriott.com/nycbx) when searching for dates. In addition to booking online with the code, the discount is available when making reservations over the phone. All that you need to mention is “Albert Einstein” or “Yeshiva University” when speaking to a reservation agent. If you’re staying in Manhattan, please see https://www.einstein.yu.edu/visitors/ (Look under Relevant Links for Hotel – lower right of page)

Friday Afternoon Discussion Group Hosted by the Office of Diversity Enhancement
Click to watch a video
Beginning with September 21, on FRIDAY afternoons at 2:30, the student representatives of Latinx Medical Student Association ( LMSA) and the Student National Medical Association ( SNMA), the Muslim Students Association (MSA), and PRIDE (LGBTQI) invite all visiting applicants to a student-led discussion, sponsored by the O ce of Diversity Enhancement, about their experiences and passions here at Einstein. It’s an opportunity to get “real talk” about unique experiences of students in an informal and candid environment. If you would like to join the discussion, please do the following:
  • Select a Friday, morning interview (or join us when you can if you’re in the area). The Friday dates that there will not be interviews, or discussion groups are: November 23, December 21, December 28, April 19 and April 26.
  • RSVP at GOOGLE forms
  • Indicate your particular interest group (LMSA, SNMA, MSA, PRIDE) or ALL. The discussion takes place at 2:30 and usually lasts about one hour. The location is the Price Center, across the street from the Admissions Office. Please leave yourself enough travel time.
Also, if you would like to meet with a member of the Office of Diversity Enhancement on the day of your interview, please email Ms. Madeline Ebanks at madeline.ebanks@einstein.yu.edu

On Interview Day
Check-In
If your interview is off-campus, and in the morning, you may opt to travel DIRECTLY there, and not check-in first in the Admissions Office. A few days before your interview, we will send you specific instructions on how to get there. You need only email us back your travel/check-in preference. We suggest you take public transportation when traveling off-campus ( Montefiore Hospital) because parking can be difficult. If you prefer, you can check in at the Admissions Office first on the Einstein campus so that you can park your car here and take the shuttle from here to your interview. Whatever you decide, an Einstein shuttle bus (marked Allways Transportation) will be available to pick you up when your interview is over, and return you to the College for the luncheon and tour which occurs around noon. Your one-way/return shuttle bus pass will be sent to you via email once you confirm your check-in preference to mary.pace@einstein.yu.edu a few days before you arrive. Please be sure to have your shuttle bus pass with you or you may not be allowed on the shuttle. Applicants who have an off-campus interview in the afternoon may depart the College directly from the interview. If you decide to come rst to the College in the morning, please check in at the Belfer Building on the corner of Morris Park Avenue and Eastchester Road, Room 206 on the second floor (directly opposite the elevator to the right). When you enter the building, please give the security guard your name and explain that you are going to the Admissions Office on the second floor.
Office Campus Interviews
Although most interviews occur on this campus, from time to time, some faculty members prefer to interview at their off-campus location. If so, you will be notified a few days in advance. We haven’t lost an applicant yet, so please do not be concerned if your interviewer is located elsewhere! Many applicants prefer the off-campus interview to have the opportunity to see one of our major clinical a liates, Montefiore Hospital ( map).
Shuttle Schedule Luncheon and Tour with Einstein Students
Our first- and second-year students will be your hosts for lunch, sometime around noon (we can let you know the exact time on that day or the day before; just contact bridget.sweeney@einstein.yu.edu. The luncheon is in the Upper Max and Sadie Lounge in the Forchheimer Building. They will meet you at the second-floor waiting area next to the Admissions Office. You might have some down time to relax, so please bring some good reading material. We do have lots of things for you to do however. You can join our students for a first- or second-year lecture; they are always happy to talk with applicants, so please give serious thought to joining them in class. Mary Pace will be happy to provide you with the class schedule once you arrive.
Self-Guided 10 minute Walking Tour Changing Room
First, in order to make your travel to and from the College easier, we have a special changing room (that accommodates one person at-a-time) that applicants may use to get ready for the interview, and / or to use when you leave, so the trip back can be more comfortable. If you decide to avail yourself of this accommodation, please allow extra time. Your greeter, Ms. Mary Pace, will direct you to the changing room.
Snacks
We will have coffee, tea, water and a variety of snacks waiting for you throughout the day. If you have a morning interview, your day will conclude around 2:00 p.m.; afternoon interviews conclude around 5:00 p.m. Please keep this in mind when making your travel plans.
Wi-fi Access
You can bring your laptop with you. Einstein has wireless Internet access but you must have WI-FI wireless networking capability. When you arrive, you may log in to YU GUEST.
